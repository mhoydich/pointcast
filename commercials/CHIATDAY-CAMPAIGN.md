## 0. Tone of this document

Chiat/Day at its peak didn't write decks. It wrote manifestos and then
one-page arguments for single images. This document follows that form.
Short paragraphs. Headlines that have to earn their line break. One idea
per page. If you can replace a sentence with a silence, do.

## 1. Situation

PointCast is a real broadcast from one real place. El Segundo,
California. 90245. It has been live since January 2025. It is built by
one person with two AIs. Every piece of content is a Block — a JSON
file with a permanent ID. Nine channels. Eight types. Three meshes:
local, online, agent. The site is addressable at both URLs and
endpoints. Every page has a machine-readable twin.

It runs on Tezos. It funds itself. It does not sell attention.

And yet, in 2026, it competes with platforms whose entire business is
synthesized feeds assembled by systems that have never been to a beach.

**The problem is not visibility. The problem is category.**

PointCast isn't a publication. It isn't a blog. It isn't a Web3
project. It isn't an AI site. If we let anyone else name the category,
we lose.

**We name it. We call it broadcasting.** Because that is the oldest,
most honest word for what it is.

## 2. The big idea

# BROADCASTING.

One word. Present tense. Active. Unfinished.

The campaign platform is that single word. It appears on every
execution. It is the noun, the verb, and the invitation. When you see
it, you know who's saying it and where.

Secondary line, when the medium allows:

### FROM EL SEGUNDO, CALIFORNIA.

Tertiary, for the rare occasion that needs a tagline under the logo:

### *The signal is alive.*

That's the whole system. Everything below is just weather.

---

## 3. Positioning

We are not positioning against other websites.
We are positioning against the *algorithm.*

The algorithm is a personalized silence. It tells you what you already
think. It never sends a real transmission.

PointCast is the opposite of the algorithm. It is one voice, broadcast
on purpose, from a fixed point on the map, that everyone on Earth can
tune to at the same URL. You don't get a version of it. You get it.

**Old idea, made new:** the broadcast.
**Old antenna, new station:** 90245.

## 5. Execution 01 — The Signal
### Hero film · :60 · Veo 3.1 · see `VEO3-80S-KAIJU-COMMERCIAL.md`

The Super Bowl spot.

A Heisei-era kaiju rises from the Pacific off Manhattan Beach at golden
hour. As it wades ashore, every rooftop antenna in El Segundo lights up
in sync. It isn't attacking. It's *carrying* — a broadcast tower on its
back, transmitting. The monster is the signal.

The whole spot is one sentence, delivered in four acts: rise, wade,
transmit, cut to card.

**End card:**

```
BROADCASTING.
pointcast.xyz
```

No logo lockup. No URL flourish. No second tagline. We let the work
breathe.

**Production notes:** full prompt pack in
[`VEO3-80S-KAIJU-COMMERCIAL.md`](./VEO3-80S-KAIJU-COMMERCIAL.md).
Cost at fal.ai Veo 3.1: ~$40 for the 3-variants-per-shot pass.

**Media:** YouTube pre-roll (30s cut-down + 60 full), Instagram Reels,
X-video, IG Story sequence, one :60 paid placement somewhere that
matters (NBA Finals, an Apple keynote feed, wherever the audience we
want is already paying attention).

## 6. Execution 02 — The Shack
### Counter-anthem · :30 · live action or Veo 3.1

The kaiju spot is large on purpose. The Shack is small on purpose.

**Open:** a single-room beach shack in El Segundo. Cedar shingles.
Rust. One satellite dish on the roof, pointed at the Pacific. Late
afternoon light through a dirty window.

**Inside:** a desk. A vintage Telefunken receiver humming at idle. A
notebook open to a page that reads **0343**. A hand (Mike's, uncredited)
writes the next title. Cross-fade to the same ID appearing at
pointcast.xyz/b/0343 on a phone on the windowsill.

**VO (pirate-radio register, warm baritone, not an announcer):**

> One person.
> One place.
> Nine channels.
> Still broadcasting.
>
> From El Segundo, California.
> You can tune in at pointcast.xyz.

**End card:**

```
BROADCASTING.
pointcast.xyz
```

The spot is :30. It uses :12 of silence. The silence is the point.

**Production notes:** can be shot live in one afternoon on a Sony FX3
with a 24mm prime. Budget: under $4k. Or rendered with Veo 3.1 — prompt
structure is in the sibling doc template. The live version is better
if Mike will sit for it.

## 10. Execution 06 — Block Drops
### Social · always on

PointCast already publishes a Block per day on the site. The campaign
twin publishes the same Block to Instagram, X, and Farcaster as a single
square card that looks nothing like a generic social post.

**Card template:**

```
┌──────────────────────────────────────┐
│                                      │
│        CH.FD  ·  № 0342              │
│                                      │
│    The headline of the block,        │
│    set in Mercury display at 64      │
│    on 70 leading, two lines max.     │
│                                      │
│                                      │
│        — pointcast.xyz/b/0342        │
│                                      │
└──────────────────────────────────────┘
```

No imagery. Black ink on warm cream. A tiny red square in the top-left
for channel color. Every card looks like it came from the same printing
press — because it did.

**Caption, always identical:**

> Broadcasting. pointcast.xyz

**Frequency:** once per Block. Current cadence is ~1.5 Blocks/day.

**Why this works:** it makes PointCast visually inseparable from itself
across every platform. You don't need to read the handle to know whose
it is. Same play as how Celine's grid looks like Celine's grid.

## 11. Execution 07 — The Dockweiler Transmitter
### Experiential · one weekend · one transmitter

**The stunt:**

For one weekend in August 2026, PointCast sets up a functional
low-power AM broadcast station on the sand at Dockweiler State Beach.
40 watts. Part 15 compliant. Legal. Transmits a five-hour loop
containing every text block from PointCast, read aloud by a rotating
cast of El Segundo residents. Reach: ~one mile of coastline.

A single hand-painted wooden sign on the sand reads:

```
POINTCAST
1620 kHz
LIVE FROM THIS BEACH
```

Anyone within a mile of the installation with an AM radio in their car
can pick it up. Surfers, joggers, the Dockweiler fire pit crowds after
dusk. The transmitter is the spot. No photographer. No "content team."
Word of mouth and one subtle Instagram post from the PointCast account
with only the coordinates and the frequency.

**Cost:** ~$2,500 (FCC Part 15 transmitter, generator, tent permit,
hand-painted sign, food for the readers).

**Why:** Chiat/Day's DNA is the brave one-weekend execution that
validates the whole campaign's claim. If we say PointCast is a
broadcast, we broadcast it — literally. Once. As a proof.

**Captured artifact:** one single-take :90 documentary shot on Super 8,
transferred, pinned at pointcast.xyz/dockweiler as Block 0XXX. The
campaign publishes its own evidence.

## 12. Execution 08 — Typographic system

One typeface across the entire campaign: **Mercury Text G1** (or
**GT Super Text** as the open-source substitute if the license doesn't
allow).

- Display — Mercury Display Bold, all caps, -2% tracking.
- Body — Mercury Text G1 Roman, 10.5/15.
- Technical — Söhne Mono, 9/12, for any block-id / URL / frequency
  treatments.

No second typeface. No system fonts. No wildcards. The type **is** the
brand identity — the way *Think Different*'s Apple Garamond and
*Chiat/Day Venice* studio signage were.

**Grid:** 12-column on every poster and layout, but every execution
occupies one column (centered) or twelve columns (full-bleed image).
Never anything in between. The negative space is the art direction.

## 14. Execution 10 — Sonic logo

:02 stinger. Used at the end of every film and radio spot.

**Construction:** one open sustained chord on a Fender Rhodes Mark II
(D major, held 1.8s), followed by a single human whistle (a descending
perfect fifth from A to D, unquantized, recorded once by a real human
standing outside the PointCast window with waves in the background),
then the word *broadcasting* said plainly, unmixed, once, by the same
voice as Execution 03.

No sweetening. No compression beyond what tape naturally does. The
stinger should sound recorded, not produced.

**File:** `/assets/sonic-logo-v1.wav` (mono, 48kHz, 24-bit, -14 LUFS)

## 19. Metrics

We measure three things and nothing else.

1. **Attention arrived of its own accord.** Direct traffic to
   pointcast.xyz/broadcasting during the campaign window, not
   referrer-attributed. If people are typing the URL, we won.
2. **Cited by agents.** Appearances of PointCast in AI summaries and
   citations — measurable via Perplexity source logs, ChatGPT cited
   URLs, Claude search results. Tracked via the existing
   `X-Agent-Mode` middleware logs.
3. **Blocks authored about the campaign by people who are not us.**
   Guest blocks, inbound references, zine mentions, unsolicited posts
   with the word "broadcasting" in them. This is the cultural metric.
   Hardest to move. Most important.

We do not measure CTR, CPM, engagement rate, impressions, or follower
growth. Those metrics describe the algorithm, not the broadcast.

## 23. Post-script — why Chiat/Day is the right lineage

Chiat/Day worked because it treated audiences like adults and products
like honest objects. Its best work — the one-image print, the single
idea TV spot, the brave stunt — all came from the same rule: **trust
that one real thing, well-shown, will travel.**

PointCast is already a real thing. It publishes itself honestly. It
doesn't need a narrative invention. It needs a campaign that believes
what the site already knows.

That's what this is.
