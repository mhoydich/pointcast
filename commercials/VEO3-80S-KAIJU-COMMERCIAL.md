# PointCast x 80s Kaiju x SoCal Commercial

## Top-Level Creative Concept

The commercial opens like a lost 1989 Toho beach-broadcast transmission recorded off late-night UHF: VHS tracking noise, heavy marine layer, synth brass, and a Japanese-style title card over the Pacific at sunset. Off the coast of El Segundo, the water starts behaving like a living signal. Foam pulses in concentric rings. Rooftop antennas tremble. The ocean is not threatening the town; it is tuning it.

From the Pacific rises a friendly rubber-suit kaiju built in the Heisei-era practical-effects language: wet latex skin, miniature-wave tanks, mist, backlight, chromatic fringing, and a heroic low-angle suitmation performance. Instead of spines or weapons, the kaiju carries a warm analog broadcast tower and antenna array on its back. As it wades toward Dockweiler Beach, every TV antenna across El Segundo lights in sync, turning the city into a mesh of glowing receivers.

The point is not destruction. The monster is the signal. PointCast becomes a living broadcast from El Segundo: local, strange, warm, analog, public, and carried by a creature that feels like community radio crossed with South Bay surf culture.

## Music Direction

Score like a late-80s Toho kaiju cue filtered through a South Bay pirate-radio transmitter: Reijiro Koroku-style brass tension and ceremonial low-end, with hints of Akira Ifukube's weight in the percussion but lighter, friendlier, and less militaristic. Instrumentation should emphasize low brass, timpani, taiko-like toms, analog Juno/Jupiter pads, spring reverb, tape hiss, shortwave radio static, and a final clean station-ID tone.

Keep the monster theme heroic and warm. The track should feel like a creature surfacing as a neighborhood broadcast, not a disaster warning.

## Stitching Notes

Cut Clip 1 to Clip 2 on the first clean underwater thump, using a 4-frame VHS tracking tear instead of a modern dissolve. Let the title card occupy 0:02-0:04, then disappear before the ocean bulge so the reveal stays clean.

Cut Clip 2 to Clip 3 on the third antenna pulse from the kaiju's back. Match the pulse to the first rooftop antenna lighting up, so the audience understands the creature is transmitting into the town.

Overlay minimal PointCast type only in Clip 3: small off-white lower-left bug reading `POINTCAST / EL SEGUNDO SIGNAL TEST`, with slight CRT jitter. Do not place web UI over the kaiju reveal.

Clip 4 should carry the only full brand lockup. Let the kaiju fill 0:24-0:28, then hard cut to black for 0:28-0:32. End card type should flicker once, settle, and hold long enough for `pointcast.xyz` to read.

Continuity anchors across clips: same kaiju head shape, same antenna tower silhouette, same three-pulse signal rhythm, same seafoam/pink/amber signal color palette, same VHS grain, same letterbox.
